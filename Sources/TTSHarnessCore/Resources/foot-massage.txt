After a 12-hour shift at St. Mary's, I slumped onto the sofa at 6:47 p.m., kicked off my Dr. Martens, and peeled off the damp cotton socks I'd worn since 5:30 a.m. My heels ached; three toenails were mauve from the new Vans I got on sale for $47.99 (regular price £129.00). I poured a mug of chamomile tea, squeezed peppermint lotion from a 200 mg tube my mother-in-law had mailed, and rubbed it slowly onto the balls of my feet, starting with the 5th metatarsal. "Ahh…" I sighed, working small circles at roughly 3 Hz, 2:1 pressure-to-release. A podiatrist, Dr. Saoirse O'Malley, once told me, "If you treat your feet like royalty, they'll carry you to the 100th mile." Mr. McAllister, who runs the reflexology clinic at Apartment 4B in building 12C on East 57th Street, NYC, charges €65 for 45 minutes and calls it state-of-the-art. The room sits at 98°F; next Tuesday, the 23rd, is 60% booked. I exhaled, pressed harder into the plantar fascia, and decided the dishes — and Jane's three unread voicemails — could wait until tomorrow.
