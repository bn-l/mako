I didn't notice how quiet the house had become until the kettle started to boil. A minute earlier, I could hear the rain tapping against the windows, the floorboards shifting softly, and the low hum of the refrigerator in the next room. Then, all at once, everything seemed to pause. I set my book down, walked to the kitchen, and poured the water slowly into a blue ceramic mug. The steam rose in a thin, twisting line, carrying the scent of tea and something faintly sweet. For a moment, standing there in the warm light, it felt like the entire evening had decided to slow down with me.
